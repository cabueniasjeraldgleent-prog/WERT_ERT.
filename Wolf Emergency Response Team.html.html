Preamble

We, the members of the Wolf Emergency Response Team (WERT), a non-governmental and non-profit emergency medical response organization, committed to the preservation of life, protection of health, and service to humanity, do hereby establish this Constitution and By-Laws.

Guided by the principles of humanitarian service, discipline, integrity, professionalism, and excellence, we bind ourselves to uphold the highest standards in emergency response, disaster management, and community service.

The Constitution

I Name and Nature

Section 1. Name

The name of the organization shall be Wolf Emergency Response Team (WERT), hereinafter referred to as the "Organization."

Section 2. Nature

The Organization is a non-stock, non-profit, non-governmental organization (NGO) dedicated to:

  • Emergency medical services
  • Disaster response
  • Community health initiatives

It operates independently but may collaborate with government agencies, private institutions, and partner organizations.

II Vision, Mission, and Objectives

Vision

To be a highly capable, trusted, and community-based emergency response organization, recognized for excellence, reliability, and humanitarian service.

Mission

To provide rapid, organized, and compassionate emergency medical response, while promoting preparedness, resilience, and public safety.

Objectives

a

Provide emergency medical assistance during disasters, incidents, and public events

b

Conduct First Aid, CPR/AED, and disaster preparedness training

c

Support Local Government Units (LGUs) and response agencies

d

Promote health awareness and community resilience

e

Develop skilled, disciplined, and ethical responders

f

Maintain readiness for rapid deployment operations

III Legal Capacity and Powers

The Organization shall have the authority to:

a. Enter into MOAs, partnerships, and agreements
b. Accept donations, grants, and sponsorships
c. Acquire and manage equipment, supplies, and assets
d. Conduct trainings, drills, and emergency operations
e. Establish and enforce internal rules and disciplinary measures
f. Represent the Organization in official engagements

IV Membership

Section 1. Eligibility

Age: At least 18 years (or with parental consent if minor)

Character: Good moral character, no misconduct record

Fitness: Physically and mentally fit for emergency response

Training: Willing to undergo training and probation

Commitment: Agree to abide by the Constitution, By-Laws, rules, and regulations

Section 2. Classification of Membership

Active Members

Completed all training, qualified for deployment and full participation

Probationary

Undergoing training with limited rights until full acceptance

Volunteer

Render voluntary service without full membership obligations

Honorary

Recognized for outstanding support, may not be operational

Section 3. Rights of Active Members

  • • To vote and be elected to any position
  • • To participate in all official activities
  • • To receive training and certifications
  • • To be informed of organizational matters

Good Standing Requirements

  • Comply with attendance requirements
  • No pending disciplinary action
  • Active participation in organizational activities

By-Laws of WERT

I Duties of Officers

Executive Leadership

Team Leader

Ultimate authority and command. Responsible for high-level decision-making, external representation, and presiding over meetings.

Deputy Leader

Primary assistant to Team Leader. Assumes all powers and duties in the Leader's absence, incapacity, or resignation.

Operational Staff

Operations Officer

Strategic planning and tactical execution of deployments

Medical Officer

Medical protocols and patient care standards

Training Officer

Curriculum design and skills evaluations

Logistics Officer

Equipment acquisition and inventory management

Communications Officer

Communication infrastructure, signal logs, inter-agency coordination, and incident reporting

Administrative Staff

Secretary

Custodian of non-financial records, meeting minutes, membership roster

Treasurer

Custodian of funds, financial reporting, budget preparation

II Membership Process

Comprehensive systematic process to ensure competence, discipline, and commitment to humanitarian service.

1

Recruitment & Application

  • Expression of Interest
  • Submission of requirements (Application Form, Valid ID, Medical Clearance, Parental Consent for minors)
2

Screening & Validation

  • Initial Screening
  • Background Check (if applicable)
3

Interview

Panel Interview assessing communication skills, attitude, willingness to serve, and readiness

4

Training & Probation

  • Mandatory Training: Basic First Aid, CPR/AED, Emergency Response Protocols
  • Probationary Period: Serve as Probationary Member, demonstrate discipline and teamwork
5

Evaluation

Performance Assessment based on skills, attendance, behavior, and deployment readiness

6

Acceptance & Oath

  • Final Approval by Team Leader
  • Oath-Taking Ceremony to become Active Member
7

Continuous Development

Ongoing training, certification upgrades, drills, and maintaining operational readiness

Grounds for Termination

• Misconduct: Improper, unethical, or unprofessional behavior
• Violation of Rules: Non-compliance with Constitution or SOPs
• Absenteeism: Unjustified absence from meetings/deployments
• Prejudicial Actions: Acts damaging WERT's reputation

Due Process Requirement

No suspension/expulsion without strict observance of due process:

  1. Written Notice of Charge - specifying complaint and facts
  2. Opportunity to be Heard - written explanation and/or appearance
  3. Investigation and Evaluation - fair, impartial, thorough
  4. Deliberation and Recommendation by disciplinary body
  5. Decision and Approval - formal written decision
  6. Notice of Decision - formal written notification

Safeguards: Impartiality (no involved officer in decision), Right to Appeal (7-15 days), Confidential documentation

III Code of Conduct

1. Professionalism & Integrity

  • Honesty: Truthful in all reports and communications
  • Conduct: Decorum expected of emergency responders

2. Patient Confidentiality

  • Non-Disclosure: Strict confidentiality of patient records
  • Media Policy: No unauthorized photos/videos of patients

3. Chain of Command

  • Follow all lawful orders promptly
  • Address grievances after mission completion

4. Ethical Use of Authority

  • No abuse of power for personal gain
  • Resources used solely for intended purposes

5. Reputational Management

  • Only authorized PIO speaks to media
  • No action undermining team reputation

6. Operational Safety

  • Maintain physical & mental fitness
  • Self-report unfitness for duty
  • Proper equipment care

7. Substance Use - Zero Tolerance

Prohibited from duty while under influence of alcohol, illegal drugs, or impairing medication. No alcohol while in uniform.

8-10. Additional Provisions

  • Social Media: No implied WERT endorsement of controversial views
  • Photography: Unauthorized scene photography prohibited
  • Harassment: Professional environment free from discrimination
  • Duty to Report: Obligation to report violations

IV Uniform and Identification

👔 Official Uniform

Required during trainings, deployments, and authorized activities. Must be worn properly and completely.

🪪 Identification

Official ID must be displayed at all times while on duty. Proof of authority for partner agencies.

✂️ Grooming

Proper grooming, cleanliness, and professional appearance. Neat and presentable uniforms.

⚠️ Proper Use

Uniform and ID only for authorized activities. Unauthorized use, misuse, or representation for personal gain strictly prohibited.

📋 Accountability

Members responsible for care and security. Loss or damage must be reported immediately.

VI Discipline and Sanctions

Progressive Discipline Model

1
Verbal Warning

Minor infractions. Record kept for 6 months.

2
Written Warning

Repeated minor or significant single violation. Specific breach documented.

3
Suspension

Temporary removal from all activities. For serious misconduct or during investigation.

4
Expulsion

Permanent termination. Reserved for egregious breaches, safety violations, or criminal activity.

Team Leader and Board reserve right to bypass steps for severe violations (criminal acts, gross negligence).

Due Process Rights

Notification

Written notice of specific charges and evidence

Right to be Heard

Formal hearing with Executive Leadership

Impartial Review

No involved officer as sole decider

Documentation

Incident reports, action plans, final decisions

Appeals Process

Members facing Suspension or Expulsion may appeal to Board/Appeals Committee within 7 days. Appeals Committee decision is final and binding.

VII Data Privacy and Confidentiality

Protected Information

  • Patient Care Records (PCRs): Medical history, treatment, physical condition
  • Personally Identifiable Information (PII): Names, addresses, contact details, IDs
  • Operational Data: Secure locations, radio frequencies, security protocols

Access and Authorization

  • 🔒 Principle of Least Privilege - Access restricted to necessary personnel
  • 🔒 Medical Officer as primary custodian of patient records
  • 🔒 Credential Security - Members responsible for login security

Storage and Security

  • 📁 Physical: Locked filing cabinets in secure facility
  • 💾 Digital: Encrypted platforms, password-protected, backed up
  • 🗑️ Retention: Secure shredding and permanent deletion

Survival of Confidentiality

The obligation to maintain confidentiality is permanent. Duty remains even after resignation, expulsion, or organizational dissolution.
